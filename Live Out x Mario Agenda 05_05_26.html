Two core reasons were causing us to still do attendance manually on a daily basis:

01

Inaccurate Address Data

The "Address Area" field used to determine if the maid resides in Satwa is unreliable, requiring manual address verification before invitations are sent.

Capacity Overflow

With Client (Not Picked Up)

With-Client (Not Picked Up) Maid Communication

Current Flow

Maids who are With Client (Not Picked Up) are not coming to the center. We wanted to look into bringing them, assuming their presence at the accommodation was necessary for hygiene preparation and delay prevention.

Client Survey

After surveying clients who received placements in the past week, zero complaints were recorded related to maid hygiene or arrival delays when maids commuted directly from home.

Recommended Policy

Keep With Client (Not Picked Up) maids at home. Their presence at the accommodation reduces actual availability without adding operational value.

Follow-up via chat can happen to make sure the maid has no problem joining her customer without physically bringing her to the accommodation.

Section 03

Automation of the Relocation Flow

01

Automatic loss-making status

Maids are automatically set as loss making as soon as they are untagged from customers outside of Satwa.

02

To-do and center meeting

A to-do is created and the maid is invited to the center to speak with the dabbour face to face.

03

Dabbour communication

If the maid refuses, she is automatically set as Pending for Termination. If she agrees, the loss-making period stays active until the deadline.

04

No-show follow-up

If the maid does not show up to the accommodation, the assigned dabbour will call her.

05

Extension policy by score

Scores 0 to -5 receive a one-week extension if requested. Positive scores receive 2-week extensions, one at a time if requested.

06

Address sharing over bot

If the maid shares her address over the chatbot within her Loss Making period, she is invited to next day attendance and marked as available again in the accommodation.

Section 04

Reducing restrictions on same day bookings

We previously had restrictions that did not allow customers to book same day taxis after 12:00 PM.

Time Restrictions on Taxis

Taxis need 2 hours to reach customers, and we use a 2 hour buffer before booking to account for maid briefing. We reduced that before-booking taxi buffer from 2 hours to 1 hour as the briefing process does not require 2 hours, and pushed the restriction from 12:00 PM to 4:00 PM to maximize utility of maid presence at the center.

Impact

Our peak tagging hours are 12:00 PM and 2:00 PM after the previous 12:00 PM cutoff. By cutting the buffer from 4 hours to 2 hours and pushing the last taxi to 4:00 PM, customers can now place same-day bookings during the highest-demand window of the day.

Section 06

No-Show Deductions Policy

Without the free bus option, late arrivals to the Satwa accommodation are a risk. A new deduction policy creates accountability while keeping the process fair and consistent.

01

Maid Is Late to Accommodation

If a maid misses the check-in window, she is marked as No Show and not invited back to the accommodation the same day.

02

12-Hour No Show Window

The No Show status holds for 12 hours, triggering an automatic salary deduction for that day.

03

Next-Day Re-Invitation

The maid is automatically invited to the accommodation the following day to be marked present and reinstated.
